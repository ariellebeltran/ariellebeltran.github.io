    • Introduction

    Advisor Link is an advising tool for undergraduate SFU students. In 2023 it had limited landing pages for students in several faculties and programs.

    Objective

    As a junior UX/UI designer, I was tasked with the following:

  • Design and create numerous landing pages for the Back On Track program, Beedieâ€™s Beedie Success Program, International Students LiveChat, and many other programs.

    • Process

    Using the Main Landing Page as a Guide

    Before I started working on the rest of the pages for Advisor Link, I was instructed to use the layout of the landing page as a guide. This allows for users of the site to familiarize with the information hierarchy and the functional (Call-To-Action) and informative tiles with ease thus lessening their cognitive load.

    Programs

    Back On Track Program

    In this program, students that fall below the minimum GPA requirements of SFU receive the Required to Withdraw status. To get the Good Academic Standing status, students must complete the Back On Track program, to improve their skills and work towards gain academic success.

    Hierarchical-Column & Tiles Usage Rationale: Visibly showing important information (Schedule, Simple-Step-Process, Success Plans) that the user can see and interact with.



    Beedie Success Program

    For this program, students that fall below the SFU Beedie GPA requirements can not enroll in upper division business courses until they meet with an advisor to come up with a study plan. This page provides them the resources, advisor contact information, and the success plans to get them back on track in the Beedie program.

    Hierarchical-Column & Tiles Usage Rationale: Mainly showing the essential information (Schedule, FAQs) and relevant actions (View Success Plans) that the user can interact with.



    ISA LiveChat

    As for international students, this landing page has a live chat feature implemented in the page. The main purpose of this page is not only to house the livechat, but to also provide additional information such as scheduling and FAQs.

    Hierarchical-Column & Tiles Usage Rationale: Mainly to keep information display as minimalistic and simple as possible.

    In Retrospect

    After making the landing pages for some of SFU's programs and comparing them back to the main landing page, it feels like the potential flow of thought and interaction for new and current users is consistent with what the pages of Advisor Link has to offer. And that is important for students of the site to experience as they mainly use it as an advising tool that connects them with advisors and view resources that can help them in their programs.

    In terms of Usability Heuristics, the relevant principles that can be applied are: Recognition Rather than Recall (less cognitive recall), Flexibility and Efficiency of Use (providing shortcuts), and Aesthetic and Minimalist Design (mainly essential information) to allow for a smooth and straightforward experience for users.
